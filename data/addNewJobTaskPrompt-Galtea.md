## Products and Technology

Galtea’s core offering is a **SaaS platform for AI evaluation and governance**.  In simple terms, it lets companies plug in their AI or large language models and automatically run a suite of tests and metrics on them.  Key components of the Galtea platform include:

* **Web-based dashboard**: An intuitive GUI (built with React) where teams can upload models or datasets, configure tests, and visualize results. Users can see side-by-side comparisons of model outputs, accuracy metrics, and compliance checks.
* **Customizable test suite**: Users can define *business-specific metrics* – not just generic benchmarks. This means a retailer can test a chatbot for retail-specific accuracy, or a bank can test a model’s financial advice for regulatory compliance. As Galtea puts it, teams “build a high-quality, customized testing stack,” ensuring each model iteration is “rigorously tested with robust, business-aligned metrics” before deployment.
* **Python SDK and REST API**: A software development kit (SDK) and API allow engineers to integrate Galtea’s evaluation tools directly into their workflows. For example, a developer can write a Python script that submits a new model to Galtea and retrieves the evaluation report. This makes it easy to incorporate continuous testing into the AI development lifecycle.
* **Performance monitoring and reporting**: The platform tracks model performance over time and flags any regressions or failures.  Dashboards show metrics for accuracy, response quality, latency, and other indicators. By catching issues early, teams can “catch failures before they reach production” and ship new AI features faster and more safely.

Galtea’s technology stack is modern and scalable. The frontend is built with React (a popular JavaScript framework) to create a fast, user-friendly dashboard. The backend and SDK are written in Python, leveraging standard ML libraries to run the evaluations.  Under the hood, Galtea leverages Barcelona Supercomputing Center’s infrastructure and research: the platform can run on high-performance hardware and incorporate the latest advances in natural language processing from BSC’s Language Technologies unit. In fact, Galtea explicitly acknowledges its BSC roots: its site says the platform was “built on Barcelona Supercomputing Center’s cutting-edge research”.

Galtea benefits from access to one of Europe’s most powerful supercomputers, the MareNostrum 5 at BSC. This high-performance computing (HPC) resource allows Galtea to run large-scale AI evaluations much faster than standard servers. The image above (from the BSC supercomputer facility) illustrates the kind of cutting-edge hardware behind Galtea’s platform. By using MareNostrum’s computing power, Galtea can analyze complex models and huge datasets efficiently, which is a major technical advantage for an AI startup.

In summary, Galtea’s product suite combines a **user-friendly front end (React dashboard)** with a **robust backend (Python SDK, APIs, ML engines)**. It integrates machine learning expertise (especially around LLMs and generative models) with practical software engineering. This lets both non-technical stakeholders (through the dashboard) and developers (through code) work together on AI quality.

## Addressing Real-World Problems

Galtea exists to tackle the very real challenges that have slowed down AI adoption in industry. Generative AI models (like GPT, image generators, etc.) offer huge benefits, but they also bring concerns about accuracy, safety, and compliance.  For example, a company might worry a language model will produce incorrect or biased information, or run afoul of new EU AI regulations.  Research suggests that Europe could gain **tens of billions in GDP** from AI by 2030, but only if companies can overcome these trust and risk issues.

Galtea’s mission is to **break down those barriers**. The platform helps ensure that AI outputs are *accurate, reliable, and legally compliant*. In their words, the technology “ensures that AI models are accurate, safe and do not violate current European regulations”. It does this by running systematic tests (for example, checking whether a model’s output deviates from expected results or contains inappropriate content) and by measuring metrics aligned with real business goals. In effect, Galtea is *bridging the gap between cutting-edge AI models and real-world trustworthiness*.

Key problems Galtea addresses include:

* **Safety and Correctness**: The platform catches errors, bias, or harmful outputs in AI before they reach customers. By validating each model iteration with rigorous tests, Galtea “catches failures before they reach production” and helps launch AI features “faster with confidence”.
* **Regulatory Compliance**: European regulations (like the EU AI Act) require AI systems to be auditable and non-discriminatory. Galtea provides the technical basis for *evaluation and regulatory compliance*, as noted by BSC: it’s the first Spanish company focused on these challenges. In practice, Galtea can automate checks for compliance rules (e.g. data privacy or fairness requirements), giving legal and business teams transparency into the AI’s behavior.
* **Faster Innovation**: By automating much of the testing, Galtea dramatically speeds up the development cycle. BSC experts estimate Galtea’s methods can cut the time from model development to deployment by around **80%**. This means companies can iterate and release AI products much quicker while still ensuring quality. One testimonial notes that Galtea’s platform “reduces iteration time and ensures models perform as expected”.

In short, Galtea aims to make AI development **as dependable as software engineering**. The CTO of Galtea, Baybars Külebi, puts it succinctly: “Right now it is very easy to develop an AI product, but ... extremely difficult to guarantee that it works as intended. Our solution is indispensable to close that gap”. By offering a structured evaluation framework, Galtea helps companies move from hopeful experimentation to reliable, scalable AI solutions.
